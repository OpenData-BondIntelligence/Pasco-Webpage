Economic Development

Civilian Labor Force

In 2019, over 93.77% of the labor force aged 16 years and older were employed in Pasco, WA. The unemployment rate was under 6.2% and 15.5% of the population was living below the poverty line.

Occupations

The most common job groups, by number of people living in Pasco, WA, are Management, Business, Science, & Arts occupations. This chart illustrates the share breakdown of the primary jobs held by residents of Pasco.

Type of Workers

For fiscal year 2019 the largest class of workers in Pasco, WA were paid in salary and private wages, followed by government and self-employment.

Industries

The most common employment sectors for those who live in Pasco, are Educational Services, Healthcare & Social Assistance. This chart shows the share breakdown of the primary industries for residents of Pasco.

Income and Benefits

Households in Pasco, WA have a median annual income of $63k and a per capita income of $24k. The majority of the populations makes between $50k – $75k.

Income and Benefits

The highest amount of benefits earned in the city of Pasco, WA came from social security income. The average amount was $21,000.

Housing

In Pasco, WA the largest share of households have a property value in the $200k-$300k range. In 2019, the median property value was $199k.
